BTS (Hangul: 방탄소년단; RR: Bangtan Sonyeondan), also known as the Bangtan Boys, is a seven-member South Korean boy band formed by BigHit Entertainment.

The group consists of RM (rapper and leader), Jin (vocalist), Suga (rapper), J-Hope (rapper), Jimin (vocalist), V (vocalist), and Jungkook (vocalist).
The group debuted on June 13th, 2013 with the song "No More Dream".

One month later, they announced their fan club, A.R.M.Y. (아미).
In early 2014, BTS won “Rookie of the Year” at all the well known Korean award shows.
In 2015, following the release of “I Need U”, BTS got their very first music show win on SBS MTV’s “The Show”.
BTS won their first album of the year grand prize at the Melon Music Awards for “The Most Beautiful Moment In Life: Young Forever” in late 2016.

They did not expect to win.
In 2017, BTS was the first K-Pop group to be nominated for a Billboard Music Award for top social artist.

They won.
A few months later, following the release of Love Yourself: Her, BTS was invited to perform at the American Music Awards.

They were the first K-Pop group to perform on an American awards stage.
Following the record breaking release of “Love Yourself: Tear” in 2018, BTS became the youngest ever recipients of the “Order of Cultural Merit” award.

They were awarded by the president of South Korea for their contributions to spreading Korean culture and Language.
Later that year, they spoke at the 73rd United Nations General Assembly.
In 2019, BTS became the first K-Pop group to perform on Saturday Night Live.

Their album, "Map of the Soul: Persona", has been topping music charts worldwide and breaking records since it's April 12th release.
A.R.M.Y and BTS always work together to push the group towards unprecedented heights.
